Alcatraz Island: A Brief History

- Location: San Francisco Bay, California, USA
- Early Use: First mapped by Spanish explorers in 1775. Later used as a military fort in the mid-1800s.
- Prison Era: Became a federal penitentiary in 1934. Known as "The Rock."
- Famous Inmates: Al Capone, George "Machine Gun" Kelly, Robert Stroud (Birdman of Alcatraz).
- Reputation: Considered inescapable due to cold waters and strong currents.
- Closure: Prison closed in 1963 due to high operating costs and deteriorating facilities.
- Today: Managed by the National Park Service as part of the Golden Gate National Recreation Area.
